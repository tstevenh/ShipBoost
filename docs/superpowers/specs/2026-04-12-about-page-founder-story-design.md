# About Page Founder Story Design

## Goal

Rewrite `/about` into a stronger entity-defining page for ShipBoost.

The new page should do four jobs at once:

1. explain who built ShipBoost
2. explain why ShipBoost exists
3. frame the problem with vanity launches and dead-directory listings
4. reinforce ShipBoost as a trust-first, operator-led launch and distribution platform for bootstrapped SaaS founders

The page should feel founder-written, not corporate, and should stay aligned with `ShipBoost-Docs/shipboost-brand-system-v1.md`.

## Why This Change

The current `/about` page is clear but too thin.

It explains positioning in short blocks, but it does not yet provide:

- a real founder-origin narrative
- enough long-form context for search engines to understand the entity
- a compelling product story for skeptical founders
- a strong explanation of why ShipBoost exists beyond generic directory copy

The rewrite should make `/about` a better trust page, a better context page, and a better internal-link destination.

## Brand Constraints

The rewrite must follow the ShipBoost brand system:

- sharp
- credible
- operator-led
- practical
- founder-native
- anti-fluff

It should reinforce the existing messaging pillars:

- Trust first
- Distribution over vanity
- Built for bootstrapped founders
- Quality over junk

It should avoid generic startup copy such as:

- “empower”
- “supercharge”
- “vibrant community”
- “revolutionary”
- “game-changing”

## Voice Direction

The page should use founder voice, written in first person.

Core narrative angle:

> I built ShipBoost because too many good bootstrapped SaaS products were getting pushed into low-trust launch surfaces, vanity spikes, and dead-directory listings.

The page should sound like a founder explaining a pattern they observed and then built around, not like a polished corporate About page.

The tone should be:

- direct
- mostly serious
- warm but crisp
- bold without being loud
- polished, not theatrical

## Content Strategy

The page should be long-form, roughly 800 to 1,200 words.

It should cover:

### 1. Founder story

Explain the practical frustration behind ShipBoost:

- good SaaS products getting buried
- weak launch surfaces creating shallow spikes
- directories existing mostly as clutter
- founders needing credibility, not just traffic

This section should establish motive, not biography theater.

### 2. Product story

Explain what ShipBoost became in response:

- a launch and distribution platform
- a cleaner discovery surface
- a place where listings can keep working after launch day

This should connect the founder’s observation directly to the product design.

### 3. Problem framing

Make the contrast explicit:

- vanity launches vs real distribution
- generic directories vs curated trust
- one-day spikes vs ongoing discovery

This section should sharpen differentiation without sounding defensive.

### 4. Why ShipBoost is structured this way

Explain the product decisions:

- weekly launches
- trust signals
- curated surfaces
- cleaner discovery paths through categories, tags, and alternatives

This section should make the product feel intentional.

### 5. Who it is for

State clearly that ShipBoost is for bootstrapped SaaS founders who care about:

- trust
- credible presentation
- practical distribution
- momentum after launch

### 6. Founder signoff

End with a short founder signoff that feels personal but restrained.

Include the founder social link:

- X profile: `https://x.com/Timhrt_`

## Page Structure

Recommended flow:

1. hero with founder-led headline
2. opening section with founder story
3. section on the market problem
4. section on what ShipBoost is and why it is built this way
5. section on who ShipBoost is for
6. founder signoff with X link

The page can keep the existing `ContentPageShell` layout unless the rewrite clearly demands a different shell.

## UI Direction

No major visual redesign is required.

The main change is content depth and structure.

The page should remain consistent with the current brand and supporting marketing pages:

- monochrome-first
- structured sections
- clear hierarchy
- editorial SaaS tone

The founder signoff should be visually distinct but still restrained, likely as a final card or closing block.

## Metadata Direction

The page metadata should be updated to match the stronger entity focus.

The title and description should reflect:

- founder story
- what ShipBoost is
- who it is for

This metadata should remain concise even though the body becomes long-form.

## Internal Linking

The rewritten `/about` page should naturally support and reference nearby pages where useful:

- `/how-it-works`
- `/pricing`
- `/submit`

Links should feel editorial, not stuffed.

## Success Criteria

The rewrite is successful if:

1. the page reads like a real founder wrote it
2. it clearly explains why ShipBoost exists
3. it strengthens ShipBoost’s trust and differentiation
4. it matches the current brand system
5. it gives search engines and users a fuller entity description than the current short page

## Out of Scope

These are not part of this change:

- redesigning the full marketing site
- adding new founder profile pages
- changing global brand positioning
- changing launch logic or product behavior
